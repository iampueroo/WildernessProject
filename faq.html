General

      Q What is the emphasis on social justice about? +
    • College is a time and place to explore new things and expose ourselves to diverse issues, cultures, and people. Discussing what students may encounter during their college life will prepare them to face these issues with an open mind. This trip is intended to increase our awareness of the natural world, ourselves, our peers and, in a bigger picture, the interaction of people and their environment in other places.
      Q What if I have never been camping? +
    • Don't worry, many participants will have never been camping before. This is your opportunity to explore something new. Trip leaders will teach you all the basic skills you will need to be comfortable. The trip is one of the best ways to learn new things with the support of leaders and a new group of friends.
      Q Why is the Wilderness Project longer than other Pre-Orientation Programs? +
    • The Wilderness Project will be an experience that immerses you in nature and your backpacking community. Enough time must be taken to go beyond backpacking in order to build a srong community and reflect on our experiences. Also, we thought it'd be fun to play out in the woods for a few extra days!
      Q Why does the Wilderness Project cost more than other Pre-Orientation Programs? +
    • The program registration fee helps to cover a variety of expenses including food, leader trainings, gear acquisition, and transportation from the airport to campus and from campus to the woods.
      Q What about Financial Aid? +
    • The Wilderness Project is dedicated to providing experiential education and outdoor recreation regardless of financial means. We offer two need-based scholarships. Please contact us if the program cost is prohibitive.
      Q What kind of shape do I need to be in? +
    • Expect this to be a physically challenging trip. We encourage everyone to engage in some type of regular physical activity in preparation for the trip. While no backpacking or outdoor experience is required, please respect that hiking 8 miles per day with a loaded backpack demands that you are physically prepared. Pay particular attention to lower body strength; doing lunge exercises and situps before the trip will make those hills seem less steep.
      Q Do I need any special equipment? +
    • Check out the Wilderness Project packing list. If you dont have some of the items listed there, borrowing from a friend, relative or neighbor is always an option. If you cannot get a hold of certain items, please contact us. The Outing Club also owns a large number of backpacks and sleeping bags to lend out. If you are getting new hiking boots, wear them as much as possible before the trip to get them well-broken in. This will help prevent blisters and make hiking more comfortable.
      Q How else can I prepare for the trip? +
    • Once you have officially registered for the trip and received confirmation from the Wilderness Project, read our online participant packet and fill out the medical and policy forms.
      Q When can I move in to my room? +
    • We will return to campus by 2pm on August 25. You can move in at this time. Anyone coming to help you move in should also arrive on campus at this time. All of your belongings besides your camping gear should be shipped or driven in to arrive on the 25th.
      Q Will I be able to move in to my dorm before we leave for the Wilderness Project? +
    • The dorms will not be available for move-in when you arrive on campus. You can move into your dorm on August 25th at 2 pm when we return. Plan on bringing the essential items for the Wilderness Project and have the rest of your belongings shipped or brought to you on or after the 25th.
      Q Can I store belongings I don't need for the trip on campus while I'm on the Wilderness Project? +
    • Please bring only what you need for the trip with you when you arrive on August 18th. You should arrange for the rest of your belongings to arrive on campus upon your return.
      Q What happens if I am injured before arriving on campus and can no longer participate? +
    • If an illness or injury prevents you from being able to participate, please let us know immediately. We will accommodate your needs as best we can.
      QWhat happens if I am injured before arriving on campus and can no longer participate? +
    • If an illness or injury prevents you from being able to participate, please let us know immediately. We will accommodate your needs as best we can.
      QWhat if I have special dietary needs? +
    • If you have specific dietary restrictions, please don't hesitate to contact us and tell us about them! We will try our best to accomodate your needs.
      QWhat if I am on medication? +
    • Please list all medications (including inhalers) on your application. If you need assistance administering these prescriptions, please contact us so that we can make the appropriate accommodations. If you have a prescription for something not listed on your application (for example: you are diagnosed with strep throat 3 days before the trip), please bring a note from your doctor with you to campus.
      QHow can my parents reach me if there is an emergency at home while I am on the trail? +
    • Parents can call the Orientation Office from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, Monday through Friday at (314) 935-8350. After-hours emergency contact information will be distributed to parents before freshman arrive on campus.
